I usually don't share my personal projects, as I mainly create them for automation and problem solving or simply for fun. As the internet has been a great resource to me for development however, I'd like to give back in any way I can by providing easy‑to‑read code in projects (at least, I try!).

Alongside that, you can see how I've progressed from start to now. This may give some insight on the type of projects a starting programmer could initially build up experience from. I often see people starting on either too ambitious or simple projects, leading to a crushing lack of motivation to keep discovering. I've found programming to be frustrating at times, but when you start understanding what's going wrong to make it work, that joy always surpasses the frustration when learning.

I don't think I'll ever be truly done with learning. I may try new programming languages just to see how many similarities they have in common with the ones I already know. Frameworks may shift focus, which could be a fun change. I may discover new libraries that lead to fun and perhaps challenging new projects.
