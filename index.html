Wild West Woodworker

A school videogame project, intended to gloss over the inner workings of Unity. I prioritized mechanics above looks, so it currently looks a bit ugly.

I had a rough time working with animations, due to long flimsy work in Unity's animation editor resetting (possibly by user error). Needing a reliable method of describing animations, independent of Unity and potential updates deprecating hard work, I worked on triggering the animations by script (losing the ability to blend animations however).

I'm not sure I'll continue this project, let alone in Unity (at least at this current version), due to all the strange progress killing blockades during development. I'll check out Godot when it has proper 3D support and perhaps Unreal too.

Usually I don't share projects, as I mainly create them for fun or personal use. As the internet has been a great resource to me for development however, I'd like to give back in any way I can by providing easy-to-read code in projects (at least, I try). Aside of that, you can see how I've progressed from start to now, which may give you insight on the type of projects you should be starting on as a starting programmer, as I often see people starting on too ambitious projects, leading to a crushing lack of motivation to keep discovering. I've found programming to be frustrating at times, but when you start understanding what's going wrong to make it work, that joy always surpasses the frustration when learning.

I don't think I'll ever be truly done with learning. I may try new programming languages just to see how many similarities they have in common with the ones I already know. Frameworks may shift focus, which could be a fun change. I may discover new libraries that lead to fun and perhaps challenging new projects.
